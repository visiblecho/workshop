# Visual Identity & Positioning Research

**Purpose:** Inform prototype UI design. Define feeling, color, positioning for an AI-native trade software product.
**Buyer:** Firm owner (Meister) or senior technician. Practical, skeptical, values reliability. Not tech-forward.

---

## Site-by-Site Analysis

### DACH Trade Software

**plancraft** (plancraft.com/de-de) -- the benchmark
- Colors: Dark charcoal background (#1F1B1C), vibrant cyan/turquoise accent (#29FFBF), white text
- Hero: "Handwerker Software -- einfach, leistungsstark, unverzichtbar"
- Imagery: Real craftspeople at work, product screenshots, minimal iconography. No cartoons.
- Typography: Inter, clean sans-serif, conversational
- Feeling: **Trustworthy, Practical, Professional**
- Note: Dark mode signals "premium" and "modern" without being intimidating. The cyan accent is distinctive and energetic.

**HERO Software** (hero-software.de)
- Colors: Yellow (#f7e004) primary, dark navy, white backgrounds
- Hero: "Handwerkersoftware für Profis" / "Weniger Papierkram, mehr Handwerk"
- Imagery: Real craftspeople (carpenters, electricians, tile layers). Authentic, not stock.
- Typography: Inter, clean, bold weights for hierarchy
- Feeling: **Trustworthy, Practical, Forward-thinking**
- Note: Yellow is warm and approachable. Authentic photos build trust.

**Taifun** (taifun-software.de) -- what to move AWAY from
- Colors: Corporate blue (#005BAB), neon green accent (#B0CA00). Feels 2015.
- Imagery: Generic stock photos of smiling workers in safety gear. Information-overloaded feature grids.
- Typography: Structured but traditional layout
- Feeling: **Established, Trustworthy, Dated**
- Anti-patterns: Dense feature lists, "benefits in boxes" layout, history-first defensive positioning, neon green on corporate blue.

**SmartHandwerk** (smarthandwerk.de) -- legacy-lite
- Similar to Taifun but simpler. Blue tones, modular pricing tables.
- Feeling: **Simple, Functional, Unremarkable**
- Note: Not offensive, but nothing memorable. No emotional pull.

### US/Global Best-in-Class

**ServiceTitan** (servicetitan.com) -- category leader
- Colors: Titan Blue (#0265DC), white, light grey. Red/green/yellow for status. Dark mode sections.
- Hero: Enterprise-grade, data-focused messaging
- Imagery: Professional, structured. Diagrams, charts, dashboards over lifestyle photos.
- Typography: Sofia Pro headings, Nunito Sans body. Generous spacing.
- Feeling: **Professional, Trustworthy, Modern**
- Note: Signals "serious business platform." Less warm than Jobber/Housecall Pro. Appropriate for larger firms.

**Housecall Pro** (housecallpro.com)
- Colors: Gold/amber (#ffb706), dark navy (#08151E), white
- Hero: "Everything to run and grow your business"
- Imagery: Professional photography of tradespeople. Dashboard mockups. Business owner headshots.
- Typography: Modern sans-serif, large bold headlines
- Feeling: **Trustworthy, Empowering, Growth-focused**
- Note: Gold = warmth + premium. "Grow your business" messaging is aspirational without being intimidating. Good model for the SMB buyer.

**Jobber** (getjobber.com) -- cleanest design
- Colors: Navy blue primary, bright lime green accent, cream/off-white background with subtle texture
- Hero: "Run a stronger service business"
- Imagery: Real business owner portraits with names/companies. App screenshots. Collage-style hero.
- Typography: Inter + Jobber Pro, heavy weights (700-900) for headings. Strong hierarchy.
- Feeling: **Professional, Approachable, Empowering**
- Note: Best balance of modern design + warm approachability. Cream backgrounds and texture avoid the cold SaaS feel. Customer faces build trust.

## Patterns: What the Best Sites Have in Common

1. **Outcome headlines, not feature headlines.** "Run a stronger business" (Jobber), "Everything to run and grow" (Housecall Pro). Never "Our software has 47 modules."
2. **Real people, not stock.** Authentic craftspeople at work. Named customers with real businesses. Trust through recognition ("that looks like me").
3. **Warm accent on neutral base.** Dark or white backgrounds with one warm/bright accent color. Never corporate blue + neon green.
4. **Clean sans-serif typography.** Inter dominates. Large headings, generous spacing. No decorative fonts.
5. **Product screenshots as proof.** Show the UI early. The Meister wants to see what they'll actually use, not abstract promises.
6. **Social proof prominent.** Star ratings, customer counts, testimonials above the fold.
7. **Mobile-first signals.** Phone mockups alongside desktop. Signals "you can use this on-site."

## Anti-Patterns: What Makes a Meister Close the Tab

1. **Feature dumps.** Dense grids of 20+ features. Screams "complex, hard to learn."
2. **Stock photography.** Generic smiling people in hard hats. Feels fake. Handwerker can tell.
3. **Corporate blue + neon green.** The legacy palette. Signals "old software, new paint."
4. **History-first positioning.** "35 years of experience" as hero message. Defensive. Signals "we're the incumbent, please don't leave."
5. **No product screenshots.** If you don't show the UI, the Meister assumes it's ugly.
6. **Tech jargon.** "AI-powered", "cloud-native", "SaaS." The buyer doesn't care about the stack. They care about the outcome.
7. **English on a German product.** Even bilingual sites need perfect, natural German. Awkward translations kill trust instantly.

## Positioning

### Unique Selling Points (for the buyer)

1. **"Dein Betrieb lernt mit jedem Auftrag."** (Your business learns with every job.) The CL layer -- your quotes get smarter, your margins get better. Not because you got an MBA, but because the system learns.

2. **"Wissen von tausenden Betrieben. Für deinen."** (Knowledge from thousands of businesses. For yours.) The crowd learning moat. You're not alone. The platform knows what similar firms quote, how long jobs take, which wholesalers deliver late.

3. **"Vom Auftrag bis zur Nachkalkulation -- in einer App."** (From job to post-calculation -- in one app.) Full cycle, mobile, no back-office required.

4. **"Keine Schulung. Keine Installation. Sprich einfach."** (No training. No installation. Just talk.) Voice-first AI interaction. The Meister describes the job, the system does the rest.

### The Feeling (adjectives for design direction)

For the prototype UI, the design should feel:

1. **Vertraut** (Trustworthy) -- this is someone's livelihood. No playfulness with their money.
2. **Klar** (Clear) -- no ambiguity. Every screen has one purpose. The Meister knows what to do in 2 seconds.
3. **Ruhig** (Calm) -- not flashy, not cluttered. Confident stillness. The opposite of information overload.
4. **Warm** (Warm) -- not cold SaaS. This is a tool for people who work with their hands. Approachable.
5. **Kompetent** (Competent) -- the system knows its stuff. Numbers are precise. Suggestions are smart. No hedging.
6. **Direkt** (Direct) -- German communication culture. Say what you mean. No marketing fluff in the UI.
7. **Modern** (Modern) -- signals "future" without screaming "tech startup." The Meister should think "finally, software that doesn't look like it was built in 2005."

### Color Direction

| Element | Direction | Rationale |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **Background** | Dark charcoal or warm off-white | Dark = premium, modern (plancraft precedent). Warm white = approachable (Jobber precedent). Either works. Avoid cold white. |
| **Primary accent** | Warm amber/gold (#F5A623 range) or teal (#2EC4B6 range) | Amber = warm, trustworthy, premium (Housecall Pro, HERO). Teal = modern, fresh, distinct from legacy blue (plancraft direction). Pick one. |
| **Text** | White on dark, or dark charcoal on light | High contrast. Readability is non-negotiable for a 50-year-old Meister. |
| **Status colors** | Green (on track), amber (attention), red (problem) | Universal. Don't reinvent. |
| **Avoid** | Corporate blue (#005BAB), neon green (#B0CA00), gradients | Legacy trade software palette. The prototype must look like the future, not the past. |

### Imagery Direction

- **Product first.** The UI is the hero. Show the quote screen, the CL overlay, the margin indicator.
- **If people:** One or two high-quality photos of a Meister looking at a phone on-site. Not in a hard hat posing -- actually working.
- **Icons:** Minimal, functional. No 3D illustrations, no cartoons, no emoji.
- **Data visualization:** Clean charts for the CL benchmarks. Bar charts, not pie charts. Numbers, not decorations.

### Hero Message Candidates (for the prototype landing/login screen)

- "Dein nächstes Angebot wird besser als dein letztes." (Your next quote will be better than your last.)
- "Handwerkersoftware, die mitdenkt." (Trade software that thinks along.)
- "Weniger raten. Mehr verdienen." (Less guessing. More earning.)
